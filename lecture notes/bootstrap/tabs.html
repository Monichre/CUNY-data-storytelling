I started with Bootstrap's tabs javascript -- I just copied their code and renamed the IDs. The secret ingredient is data-toggle="tab". That sets each <div> up to toggle. It is pretty simple, really.
But as long as I have you, I want to talk about main elements.
When you pitch a story to an editor, you want to have the facts. It is one thing to say "I'm interested in writing about Olympic injuries." That's great, and it isn't a bad thing to say, but...
"Olympic injuries" isn't a story. "Curling isn't the safest sport in the games," -- that's a story. Or the start of one. So when you write your main elements, you're looking for statements of fact that stand out: what do you know that I should know?
